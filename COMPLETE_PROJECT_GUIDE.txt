  if sentiment == 'negative':

TAB 3: LSTM FORECASTING (THE MATH BRAIN)
========================================================================

WHAT IT IS:
The quantitative heart of the system. It fetches 5 years of real WTI Crude Oil price data from the stock market, trains a neural network, and outputs a 30-day forecast.

WHAT IT MEANS ON THE SCREEN:
- Metric Cards: Glowing neon numbers showing the exact current price of WTI Crude, the 30-day forecasted price, and the exact percentage change. 
- Interactive Chart: The blue line is the actual, historical stock market data. The red dashed line is what the AI predicts will happen next.

THE CODE BEHIND IT (src/price_predictor.py):
This is the most mathematically complex file, broken into 3 parts.

1. Real Data Fetcher (`generate_mock_oil_data`):
   - We use the `yfinance` library (Yahoo Finance).
   - Code: `ticker = yf.Ticker("CL=F")` and `df_raw = ticker.history(period="5y")`.
   - "CL=F" is the actual stock ticker for West Texas Intermediate (WTI) Crude Oil. 
   - *Fail-Safe:* If the internet disconnects during your presentation, a `try/except` block automatically generates fake "random walk" data so your dashboard doesn't crash.

2. Data Preparation (`prepare_data`):
   - Code: `self.scaler.fit_transform(price_data)`
   - Neural networks cannot understand raw dollar amounts like $85. We use Scikit-Learn's `MinMaxScaler` to squeeze all prices into decimals between -1 and 1. 

3. The LSTM Architecture (`OilPriceLSTM`):
   - We use PyTorch (`import torch.nn as nn`).
   - The architecture is a "Direct Multi-Output" model. 
   - Code: `self.lstm = nn.LSTM(input_size=1, hidden_size=128, num_layers=2, dropout=0.2)`
   - This creates 2 massive layers of memory cells (128 units each). The `dropout=0.2` randomly turns off 20% of the neurons during training to prevent "overfitting" (memorizing the answers).
   - Code: `self.fc = nn.Linear(hidden_size, output_days)`
   - Why this is brilliant: Older AI models predict tomorrow, then use tomorrow's prediction to predict the next day. Errors pile up, resulting in a straight flat line on the graph. Our `fc` (fully connected) layer maps the final hidden state to ALL 30 DAYS AT ONCE. This prevents error accumulation and creates the realistic jagged red line on your graph.

TAB 4: WHAT-IF SCENARIO SIMULATOR
========================================================================

WHAT IT IS:
An interactive economic modeling tool. It allows the user to apply hypothetical macroeconomic "shocks" (like an OPEC cut) to the AI's baseline prediction.

WHAT IT MEANS ON THE SCREEN:
- Sliders: You can dynamically alter OPEC Production, Global Demand, and the INR-USD exchange rate.
- Interactive Graph: The baseline LSTM prediction turns gray. As you move the sliders, a new bright orange line dynamically calculates and redraws the new reality.
- Indian Market Impact: It calculates exactly what the orange line means in Indian Rupees.

THE CODE BEHIND IT (src/dashboard.py - lines 189 to 258):
- The Sliders: We use `st.slider()` to capture user input.
- The Math Algorithm:
  ```python
  price_multiplier = 1.0 + (-opec_cut * 0.03) + (demand_shock * 0.015)
  scenario_predictions = [price * price_multiplier for price in future_predictions]
  ```
- How it works: 
  * If OPEC cuts production by 1 million barrels (`opec_cut = -1.0`), the math does `-(-1.0) * 0.03`, which is `+0.03`. It adds a 3% price surge to the baseline LSTM prediction.
  * If demand booms by 10% (`demand_shock = 10`), it adds `10 * 0.015 = 0.15` (a 15% price surge).
  * We then take every single day in the `future_predictions` array (from the LSTM) and multiply it by this `price_multiplier`.
- Plotly redrawing: The Streamlit app constantly loops. The moment you touch a slider, the Python script recalculates the `scenario_predictions` array and redraws the Plotly graph in real-time. 
- INR Translation: `(avg_scenario * inr_usd)` simply multiplies the simulated global dollar price by the current exchange rate slider to prove the project's relevance to the Indian economy.
